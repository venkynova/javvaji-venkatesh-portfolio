Fiscal Consolidation Supporting Bonds
The fiscal deficit target of 4.5% of GDP for FY26, down from 5.1% in FY24, reduces net supply pressure and is a structural positive for long-duration G-Secs.

Hold GOI 10Y Benchmark · 2034 Series

10-Year Benchmark — Fairly Valued

The 10Y benchmark is fairly priced, reflecting the current macro environment. Existing holders should hold as further rate cuts will provide price appreciation. New entrants, however, may find better risk-reward at the 5Y tenor given the flat 2Y–10Y spread.

Watch GOI 30Y G-Sec · 2054 Series
—

30-Year Bond — Avoid at Current Spread

The 30Y–10Y spread of just 18 bps is historically narrow, compensating poorly for the additional 9.3 years of duration risk. Unless the spread widens above 40 bps, the 30Y segment offers an unattractive risk-reward trade-off for most investor classes.
